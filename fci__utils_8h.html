Calculate the parity and determinant resulting from a double excitation.

The parity is (-1) raised to the power of the number of occupied orbitals separating the occupied and virtual orbitals in the excitation

Parameters
[in,out]detPointer to origin determinant. Upon return, contains new determinant
[in]orbsThe indices of the two occupied orbitals (0th and 1st elements) and two virtual orbitals (2nd and 3rd elements) defining the excitation
Returns
parity of the excitation (+1 or -1)

Identify whether 2 Slater determinants are connected by a single or double excitation.

Parameters
[in]str1First Slater determinant
[in]str2Second Slater determinant
[out]orbsThe orbitals defining the excitation. For a single excitation, orbs[0] is occupied in str1 and unoccupied in str2 ; orbs[1] is unoccupied in str1 and occupied in str2 . For a double excitation, orbs[0] and orbs[1] are occupied in str1 and unoccupied in str2 ; orbs[2] and orbs[3] are unoccupied in str1 and occupied in str2
